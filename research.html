Research
Projects, themes, and long-term directions.
Overview

Broadly, I work at the interface of commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, using algebraic, combinatorial, and geometric methods to study the asymptotic behavior of powers of ideals associated to configurations of points.

Current themes include:

  • ● Containments between symbolic, ordinary, and rational symbolic powers of ideals of points in $\mathbb{P}^n$.
  • ● Waldschmidt constants, resurgence, and related invariants for fat-point schemes.
  • ● Polyhedral and valuation-theoretic approaches (e.g., Newton polyhedra, Newton–Okounkov bodies).
  • ● Links with interpolation problems, Seshadri constants, and birational geometry.
  • Rational symbolic powers and interpolation
    Preprint in preparation, 2025 (with collaborators)
    Developing a framework for $I^{(\alpha)}$ with $\alpha \in \mathbb{Q}$ via valuations and Newton–Okounkov bodies, motivated by interpolation problems for fat points on projective spaces.
  • Asymptotic invariants of fat-point configurations
    Ongoing project
    Studying the interaction among Waldschmidt constants, resurgence, and Castelnuovo–Mumford regularity in families of general and special configurations of points.
Commutative Algebra
Symbolic powers, containments, and asymptotic invariants.
Area
  • ● Symbolic / ordinary containment problems and uniform symbolic topologies.
  • ● Waldschmidt constants, resurgence, and regularity for fat points.
  • ● Rational symbolic powers and valuation-based constructions.
Algebraic Geometry
Fat-point schemes, interpolation, and birational tools.
Area
  • ● Interpolation problems for points in projective spaces and related conjectures.
  • ● Connections with Seshadri-type invariants and geometry of blowups.
  • ● Geometric approaches to bounds and asymptotics.
Combinatorial Algebra
Polyhedral, matroidal, and valuation-theoretic viewpoints.
Area
  • ● Newton polyhedra / Okounkov bodies for powers and symbolic powers.
  • ● Matroid-flavored methods for interpolation and configurations.
  • ● Computations and experiments (e.g., Macaulay2 / Singular workflows).
