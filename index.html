I study symbolic powers, interpolation, and asymptotic invariants in algebraic geometry.

My work focuses on understanding how the geometry of fat-point schemes and blowups of projective spaces controls containment thresholds between ordinary and symbolic powers of ideals, with connections to conjectures of Chudnovsky and Demailly.

Overview

Broadly, I work at the interface of commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, using algebraic, combinatorial, and geometric techniques to study the asymptotic behavior of powers of ideals.

Current themes include:

  • ● Containments between symbolic and ordinary powers of ideals of points in $\mathbb{P}^n$.
  • ● Waldschmidt constants, resurgence, and related invariants for fat-point schemes.
  • ● Polyhedral and valuation-theoretic approaches to rational symbolic powers.
  • ● Connections with interpolation, Seshadri constants, and birational geometry.
  • Rational symbolic powers and interpolation
    Preprint in preparation, 2025 (with A. Collaborator, B. Collaborator)
    Developing a framework for $I^{(\alpha)}$ with $\alpha \in \mathbb{Q}$ via valuations and Newton–Okounkov bodies, motivated by interpolation for fat points.
  • Asymptotic invariants of fat-point schemes
    Ongoing project
    Studying the interaction between Waldschmidt constants, resurgence, and Castelnuovo–Mumford regularity in families of configurations.
