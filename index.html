Commutative Algebra · Algebraic Geometry · Teaching

I study symbolic powers, rational symbolic powers, and asymptotic invariants arising from fat-point schemes.

My research lies at the intersection of commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, focusing on how the geometry of fat-point configurations and blowups of projective spaces governs containment thresholds between ordinary, symbolic, and rational symbolic powers of ideals, with connections to conjectures of Chudnovsky and Demailly.

More about me: I am currently a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at Tulane University and will be on the academic job market, seeking research-oriented and teaching-focused positions. I am especially interested in combinatorial and geometric approaches to symbolic powers and interpolation problems, and in collaborating across commutative algebra, algebraic geometry, and related areas.
