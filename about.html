About Me
Background, motivation, and academic journey.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mathematics at Tulane University, working in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry. My research focuses on symbolic and rational symbolic powers of ideals, fat-point schemes, interpolation problems, and asymptotic invariants such as Waldschmidt constants and resurgence.

Much of my work is motivated by classical and modern questions about containments between ordinary and symbolic powers, and by conjectures of Chudnovsky and Demailly. I am particularly interested in how the geometry of multipoint blowups of projective spaces reflects subtle algebraic behaviors of these powers and their asymptotic invariants.

I am originally from Assam, India. Before joining Tulane, I completed my Master's studies at Tezpur University, where I was first drawn to the study of ideals of fat points, interpolation problems, and their connections to algebraic geometry. These early projects naturally evolved into my current work on rational symbolic powers and valuation-theoretic approaches.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to teaching and mentoring. I have served as both primary instructor and teaching assistant for a range of undergraduate courses, and I enjoy guiding students through Directed Reading Programs on topics in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry.
